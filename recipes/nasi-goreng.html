Fried rice is a versatile dish made by stir-frying cooked rice with oil, typically mixed with ingredients like eggs, vegetables, and meat or seafood. It is known for its savory flavor, slightly crispy texture, and the way each grain of rice stays separate when cooked properly. Soy sauce and aromatics such as garlic, onions, or scallions are often added to enhance its taste.

Originating in East Asia, fried rice developed as a practical way to use leftover rice and ingredients, reducing waste while creating a satisfying meal. Today, it has countless regional variations around the world, adapting to local flavors and preferences while remaining a quick, comforting, and widely loved dish.

Ingredients

  • 2 plates of cold rice
  • 2 eggs, lightly beaten
  • 3 cloves of garlic, finely chopped
  • 5 cloves of shallots, thinly sliced
  • 3 red chilies, or to taste, thinly sliced
  • 1 tsp sweet soy sauce
  • 1 tsp soy sauce (optional)
  • ½ tsp salt
  • ½ tsp ground pepper
  • 2 tsp cooking oil for sautéing
  • 1 stalk of spring onion, thinly sliced

Steps

  1. Heat oil in a wok, sauté garlic, shallots, and chilies until fragrant.
  2. Slide the seasoning to the edge of the wok, pour in the beaten eggs, and scramble until cooked through.
  3. Add the white rice and mix well with the seasoning.
  4. Add the sweet soy sauce, soy sauce, salt, and pepper.
  5. Stir until all the seasonings are evenly distributed.
  6. Add the spring onions and stir briefly until wilted.
  7. Remove and serve with crackers, pickles, and other side dishes of your choice.
